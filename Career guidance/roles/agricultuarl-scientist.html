Agricultural Scientist

Job Description

An Agricultural Scientist researches ways to improve the productivity and sustainability of farms and agricultural systems. They work on enhancing crop yields, soil health, pest resistance, and agricultural technology.

Skills Required

  • Scientific research and data analysis
  • Understanding of plant and soil science
  • Problem-solving and critical thinking
  • Knowledge of agricultural machinery and technology
  • Fieldwork and laboratory skills
  • Technical writing and communication

Educational Requirements

A bachelor’s degree in Agriculture, Agricultural Science, Biology, or related fields is essential. Advanced roles may require a Master’s or Ph.D. in a specialized agricultural field.

Duties & Responsibilities

  • Conducting research on crop improvement and soil health
  • Developing environmentally sustainable farming techniques
  • Collaborating with farmers and agricultural companies
  • Testing and developing new fertilizers, seeds, and pesticides
  • Analyzing data and publishing findings in scientific journals
  • Advising policymakers and stakeholders on agricultural practices

Average Salary

₹3,50,000 to ₹9,00,000 per year (India, depending on role and experience).

Companies Offering This Role

  • Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR)
  • Syngenta
  • Bayer CropScience
  • Mahyco
  • State Agriculture Departments
  • Agrochemical and biotech firms
