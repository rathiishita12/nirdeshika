Veterinarian

Job Description

Veterinarians diagnose and treat medical conditions in animals, both domestic and wild. They provide preventive healthcare, perform surgeries, and advise pet owners or animal handlers on proper care and nutrition.

Skills Required

  • Strong knowledge of animal anatomy and diseases
  • Excellent diagnostic and surgical skills
  • Empathy and patience with animals and their owners
  • Critical thinking and problem-solving
  • Manual dexterity and attention to detail
  • Communication and interpersonal skills

Educational Requirements

A Bachelor of Veterinary Science and Animal Husbandry (B.V.Sc & AH) is required. Advanced specialization or a Master’s/Ph.D. is beneficial for research or academic roles. A license from the Veterinary Council of India is mandatory to practice.

Duties & Responsibilities

  • Diagnosing illnesses and injuries in animals
  • Performing surgeries and administering treatments
  • Conducting regular health check-ups and vaccinations
  • Educating pet owners on animal care and nutrition
  • Maintaining detailed medical records of animals
  • Collaborating with other animal health professionals

Average Salary

₹4,00,000 to ₹10,00,000 per year (India, depending on experience and type of practice).

Organizations Offering This Role

  • Private veterinary clinics and hospitals
  • Government animal husbandry departments
  • Animal welfare organizations (e.g., PETA, Blue Cross)
  • Zoos and wildlife sanctuaries
  • Pharmaceutical and animal nutrition companies
