Radiologic Technologist

Job Description

A Radiologic Technologist performs diagnostic imaging examinations such as X-rays, CT scans, and MRIs to assist physicians in diagnosing medical conditions. They ensure patient safety, operate imaging equipment, and maintain detailed imaging records.

Skills Required

  • Proficiency in operating radiologic imaging equipment
  • Knowledge of anatomy, physiology, and radiographic positioning
  • Attention to detail and strong observational skills
  • Effective communication and patient care abilities
  • Understanding of radiation protection and safety protocols
  • Ability to follow precise technical instructions

Educational Requirements

A diploma or associate degree in Radiologic Technology or Medical Imaging is typically required. Certification or licensing from a recognized radiologic board is often necessary to practice professionally.

Duties & Responsibilities

  • Preparing patients for radiologic procedures
  • Operating imaging equipment to capture diagnostic images
  • Maintaining patient records and imaging reports
  • Ensuring radiation safety measures are in place
  • Assisting physicians in evaluating images
  • Maintaining equipment and cleanliness of imaging areas

Average Salary

₹3,00,000 to ₹7,00,000 per year (India, varies with experience, institution, and location).

Organizations Offering This Role

  • Hospitals and Diagnostic Centers
  • Private and Government Healthcare Facilities
  • Medical Imaging Labs
  • Telemedicine and Teleradiology Services
  • Medical Colleges and Universities
  • Radiology Equipment Manufacturers
