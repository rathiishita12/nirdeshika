Air Traffic Controller

Job Description

An Air Traffic Controller is responsible for coordinating the safe and orderly movement of aircraft in the sky and on the ground. They provide instructions to pilots during takeoff, landing, and while en route, ensuring minimal delays and avoiding collisions.

Skills Required

  • Excellent communication and decision-making skills
  • Strong spatial awareness and concentration
  • Ability to work under pressure
  • Multitasking and problem-solving abilities
  • Attention to detail
  • Technical and mathematical aptitude

Educational Requirements

A bachelor's degree in aviation, engineering, or a related field is recommended. Selection and training are typically conducted by national aviation authorities such as the Airports Authority of India (AAI).

Duties & Responsibilities

  • Directing aircraft during takeoff, landing, and taxiing
  • Monitoring aircraft movements via radar and other systems
  • Providing pilots with weather updates and route information
  • Coordinating with other controllers and emergency services
  • Ensuring compliance with air traffic regulations
  • Managing multiple aircraft simultaneously with precision

Average Salary

₹6,00,000 to ₹15,00,000 per year (India, based on seniority and location).

Organizations Offering This Role

  • Airports Authority of India (AAI)
  • Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA)
  • Indian Air Force
  • Indira Gandhi Institute of Aeronautics
  • Private and international airports
