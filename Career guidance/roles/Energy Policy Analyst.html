Energy Policy Analyst

Job Description

An Energy Policy Analyst researches, analyzes, and develops policies related to energy production, distribution, and consumption. They work to promote sustainable energy practices and ensure that energy regulations align with environmental and economic goals.

Skills Required

  • Strong research and analytical skills
  • Understanding of energy markets and environmental regulations
  • Policy development and evaluation expertise
  • Knowledge of renewable energy technologies and trends
  • Excellent written and verbal communication
  • Critical thinking and data interpretation skills

Educational Requirements

A bachelor’s or master’s degree in Energy Policy, Environmental Science, Public Policy, Economics, or a related field is required. Advanced knowledge in climate change and sustainability is an advantage.

Duties & Responsibilities

  • Analyzing current energy policies and their economic/environmental impacts
  • Recommending improvements to existing policies and drafting new proposals
  • Collaborating with government agencies and private stakeholders
  • Monitoring international energy trends and standards
  • Presenting findings to policymakers and the public
  • Conducting impact assessments for new energy initiatives

Average Salary

₹6,00,000 to ₹15,00,000 per year (India, varies by sector and experience).

Companies Offering This Role

  • The Energy and Resources Institute (TERI)
  • Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE)
  • World Resources Institute (WRI) India
  • Confederation of Indian Industry (CII)
  • ReNew Power
  • Consulting firms specializing in energy and sustainability
