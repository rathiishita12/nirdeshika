Marine Biologist

Job Description

A Marine Biologist studies marine organisms and their interactions with the environment. This includes research on ocean ecosystems, marine species, and the impacts of human activity on marine life.

Skills Required

  • Strong background in biology and ecology
  • Fieldwork and laboratory research skills
  • Data analysis and statistical software proficiency
  • Attention to detail and critical thinking
  • Excellent communication and reporting abilities
  • Swimming and diving skills for underwater research

Educational Requirements

A bachelor's degree in Marine Biology, Zoology, Environmental Science, or a related field is required. Advanced research roles often require a master’s or Ph.D.

Duties & Responsibilities

  • Conducting research on marine life and ecosystems
  • Monitoring environmental changes and ocean health
  • Collecting and analyzing biological samples
  • Writing scientific reports and publishing research
  • Collaborating with conservation organizations
  • Educating the public about marine conservation

Average Salary

₹4,00,000 to ₹12,00,000 per year (India, based on experience and research funding).

Companies Offering This Role

  • National Institute of Oceanography (NIO)
  • Wildlife Institute of India
  • Indian National Centre for Ocean Information Services (INCOIS)
  • WWF India
  • Marine conservation NGOs
  • Environmental consultancies
