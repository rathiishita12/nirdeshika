Urban Planner

Job Description

An Urban Planner develops plans and programs for land use in urban, suburban, and rural areas. They help communities manage growth, improve infrastructure, and ensure sustainable and efficient development.

Skills Required

  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Knowledge of zoning laws and urban development regulations
  • Proficiency in GIS and planning software
  • Effective communication and public speaking abilities
  • Project management and collaboration skills
  • Understanding of environmental and social sustainability

Educational Requirements

A bachelor's degree in Urban Planning, Architecture, Civil Engineering, or Geography is essential. A master's degree in Urban or Regional Planning is often preferred for advanced roles.

Duties & Responsibilities

  • Creating land use plans and policies for urban development
  • Analyzing population trends, infrastructure, and environmental data
  • Engaging with government bodies, stakeholders, and the public
  • Evaluating development proposals and zoning regulations
  • Promoting sustainable and efficient community growth
  • Monitoring and updating urban development plans

Average Salary

₹4,00,000 to ₹10,00,000 per year (India, varies based on experience and employer).

Companies Offering This Role

  • Town and Country Planning Organization
  • Delhi Development Authority (DDA)
  • Municipal Corporations
  • Private urban planning and infrastructure firms
  • Environmental consulting agencies
  • Smart city and infrastructure development projects
